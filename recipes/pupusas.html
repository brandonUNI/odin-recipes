Pupusas Recipe

Description

Pupusas are traditional Salvadoran stuffed corn tortillas made from masa harina (corn flour). They are typically filled with a variety of ingredients such as cheese, refried beans, and seasoned pork (chicharrón). The dough is formed into a ball, flattened, filled, and then cooked on a griddle until golden brown. Pupusas are often served with a tangy cabbage slaw called curtido and a tomato-based salsa. They are a popular comfort food in El Salvador and enjoyed by many around the world.

Ingredients

  • Masa harina (corn flour)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Queso fresco or mozzarella cheese (for filling)
  • Refried beans (for filling)
  • Seasoned pork (chicharrón, for filling)
  • Oil (for cooking)
  • Cabbage (for curtido)
  • Carrots (for curtido)
  • Onion (for curtido)
  • Vinegar (for curtido)
  • Tomatoes (for salsa)
  • Garlic (for salsa)
  • Cilantro (for salsa)
  • Salt and pepper (for salsa)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix masa harina, water, and salt to form a dough. Knead until smooth and pliable.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  3. Flatten each ball into a disc using your hands.
  4. Add a spoonful of filling (cheese, refried beans, or seasoned pork) to the center of each disc.
  5. Carefully fold the edges of the disc around the filling to enclose it, then flatten it again to form a thick, filled tortilla.
  6. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium-high heat and lightly oil it.
  7. Cook the pupusas on the hot griddle for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.
  8. Prepare the curtido by mixing shredded cabbage, grated carrots, and thinly sliced onion with vinegar, salt, and water. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  9. Make the salsa by blending tomatoes, garlic, cilantro, salt, and pepper until smooth. Cook the salsa in a pan over medium heat for 10 minutes to thicken.
  10. Serve the hot pupusas with curtido and salsa on the side.
